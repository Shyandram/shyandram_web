Master's Thesis Weakly Supervised Learning Triplet Content Loss

Master's Thesis Project (2022/10-2023/7)

My Master's thesis involved developing an **End-to-End Deep Learning Network** for generating synthetic weather images. This work is crucial for augmenting datasets used in training robust autonomous driving systems and outdoor computer vision models.

Scientific Methodology

The network employs **Weakly Supervised Learning** to translate clear weather images into high-fidelity synthetic versions of fog, precipitation, and other adverse conditions. A key technical contribution is the use of **Triplet Content Loss** to ensure that the semantic content and structure of the original image are preserved while accurately applying weather effects.

The generated images exhibit high visual realism and have been proven effective in improving the robustness of downstream object detection tasks in bad weather.
